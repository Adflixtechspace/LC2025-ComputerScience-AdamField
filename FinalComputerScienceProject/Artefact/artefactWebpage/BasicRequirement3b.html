The statistics of the CAO points from 2018 until 2024

The following figures (Fig.1 to Fig.7) show the CAO points distribution for each of their respective years.

please note that in Fig.1 -> Fig.7 the 0-100 points interval has been disregarded, so the following graphs are only histograms of those who scored over 100 ponts. This decision was made in order to genuinely reflect the change in skew over the years. Frequency measures the number of students to attain those CAO points. We can clearly see the impact of Predicted Grades during the Covid Lockdown years, when the distributions become less symmetric.

In Figs 1 & 2 above a distinct taper can be seen on the right hand side of the distribution. During the Covid years the taper down began at the same place, however there is now a stacked increase in the 600+ intervals. This trend seems to persist in the "post" covid years of 2022 and 2023. One may be forgiven for thinkg that Fig.7 is the same as Fig.6, howver if you mouse over you can see there are some differences. It does beg the question, when two data sets adopt almost the exact same shape, is there some external influence operating on the data to ensure that it conforms to the required shape. Interestingly also is to compare the graphs above to the next graph which shows how the mean, mode, and median of the number of points scored has changed over the 7 years in question.

The following figure (Fig.8) shows how the Mean, Mode and Median have changed over the past 7 years

The above graph has some interesting features, we spot that before the pandemic the means and medians were approximately 100 points below the mode, whereas during the pandemic and since the pandemic the difference between the means and the modes has shrunk to approximately 50 points, This would indicate that more people are attaining scores closer to the most common scores. One way of viewing this would be to conclude that the average student is becoming more prevalent or common. hen the mean approaches the mode, the average score is approaching the most popular score.
